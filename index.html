Glossary

CP (Centipawn) — Unit of evaluation equal to 1/100th of a pawn. +150 CP means you are ahead by ~1.5 pawns of advantage. The engine uses this to measure who is winning.

ACPL (Average Centipawn Loss) — How many centipawns you lose per move on average compared to the engine's best move. Lower is better:
• 0-30 ACPL = Excellent (GM-level)
• 30-80 ACPL = Good (club player)
• 80-150 ACPL = Average (casual player)
• 150+ ACPL = Needs improvement

Win % — Probability of winning from the current position, calculated from the CP evaluation. 50% = equal, 90% = almost certainly winning.

Move Classifications:
• Brilliant = A sacrifice confirmed as the engine's best move
• Best = The engine's top choice
• Excellent = Very close to best, loses <2% win chance
• Good = Solid move, loses 2-6% win chance
• Book = Standard opening theory move
• Inaccuracy = Slightly imprecise, loses 6-12% win chance
• Mistake = Significant error, loses 12-25% win chance
• Blunder = Serious error, loses >25% win chance
Note: Moves in already-lost positions are capped at Mistake. Moves that still leave you winning are capped at Inaccuracy.

How to Read This Report

Avg CP Loss — Average centipawns (1/100 pawn) lost per move vs engine's best. Lower = better play. GM-level is under 30; casual play is 80-150.

ACPL per Phase — Same metric broken down by game stage: Opening (moves 1-10), Middlegame (11-25), Endgame (25+). Shows where you lose the most.

Accuracy % — Percentage of moves classified as Good or Best. Above 60% is solid; above 75% is strong.

Est. Rating — Rough ELO estimate based on your play quality (ACPL). This is approximate — your actual rating also depends on your opponents.

Blunders / Game — Average number of moves per game that lose >20% win chance. Under 1.0 is good; over 2.0 needs work.

Opening Repertoire — Your most-played openings with win rate and ACPL. High ACPL on an opening means you may not understand its plans well.
